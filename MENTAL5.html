Early signs

There is no physical test or scan that reliably indicates whether a person has developed a mental illness. However, people should look out for the following as possible signs of a mental health disorder: withdrawing from friends, family, and colleagues avoiding activities that they would normally enjoy sleeping too much or too little eating too much or too little feeling hopeless having consistently low energy using mood-altering substances, including alcohol and nicotine, more frequently displaying negative emotions being confused being unable to complete daily tasks, such as getting to work or cooking a meal having persistent thoughts or memories that reappear regularly thinking of causing physical harm to themselves or others hearing voices experiencing delusions Treatment There are various methods for managing mental health problems. Treatment is highly individual, and what works for one person may not work for another. Some strategies or treatments are more successful in combination with others. A person living with a chronic mental disorder may choose different options at various stages in their life. The individual needs to work closely with a doctor who can help them identify their needs and provide them with suitable treatment. Treatments can include: Psychotherapy, or talking therapies This type of treatment takes a psychological approach to treating mental illness. Cognitive behavioral therapy, exposure therapy, and dialectical behavior therapy are examples. Psychiatrists, psychologists, psychotherapists, and some primary care physicians carry out this type of treatment. It can help people understand the root of their mental illness and start to work on more healthful thought patterns that support everyday living and reduce the risk of isolation and self-harm.
